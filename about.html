About Us

The leading Swiss organisation for hosting unconference-style events, aimed at promoting education in the data space, around topics like Data Analytics, Data Science, Business Intelligence, UX Research.

Our Purpose

The idea of our events is to create an environment for people to create, learn and share through discussions and interactions with other participants. They provide an alternative to traditional (analytics) conferences with a 1-to-many format.

As unconference-style events, there are no pre-defined speakers. You, as the participant, will provide the topics and content you find most interesting and useful.

Our events provide:

  • People from diverse backgrounds and positions in data and elsewhere to network and learn from

  • A place to discuss, create, share and collaborate on various topics that interest you

  • Low-cost opportunities to meet new people and expand your network

  • A relaxed, friendly atmosphere to both be serious and fun

Our History

AnalyticsCamp Switzerland was born from the lack of traditional education and knowledge-sharing formats, which leaves the industry to rely on other forms and means.

Furthermore, most Data Analytics conferences are designed more as a sales format with few hands-on, interactive elements that are the core of (tacit) knowledge-sharing. The dialogue of sponsor-driven events tend to revolve around tools, instead of actually talking about other important subjects within the data space.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a BarCamp exactly?

A BarCamp is a style of event, also known as a unconference. You can read more about that on our barcamp explainer page.

How do you make money?

Since our foundation we are a non-profit organisation and since 2019 officially an association (Verein). We charge a minor entry fee for larger events but smaller events tend to be free. Additionally, we have sponsors that help support us. All of our revenues are directly re-invested in making the events as low-cost and cool as possible for attendees. All association members pay yearly dues as well.

Where do you host events?

All of our events take place in Switzerland. We generally host events in the Zurich region, but also are open & planning events in other cities and regions such as Winterthur and Bern.

What about Food & Drink?

All events have water and coffee (sometimes juice & softdrinks) provided. Each event varies, but we always have a timeslot for lunch and when possible, we provide snacks throughout the day as well. For smaller events, we sometimes cover all the food (such as pizza).

Can I help out in some way?

We're currently not accepting new members into the association (Verein). However, we always welcome volunteers at each event to help us tidy up or do small administrative tasks on the day (barcamp culture).

What's the difference between AnalyticsCamp and MeasureCamp?

When we founded AnalyticsCamp in 2017, we did not yet know of the existence of MeasureCamp. We based our format on our experience of other barcamps we attended and made our own adjustments. Later, we briefly discussed about joining MeasureCamp, but decided against it due to some differences in format: (1) to be accepted as a conference in the conservative Swiss market and to make it easier for working parents (inclusion), we usually hold events during the week and not on a Saturday; (2) we do charge - albeit very little - the occasional entry fee to overcome typical Meetup-style no-show rates and reduce our dependence on sponsorship; (3) we do not simply open up the session board but do a traditional barcamp planning session to get a better overview of participants interest.
However, while we differ in format, we wholeheartedly support MeasureCamp and do occsionally attend ourselves.

What's the difference between AnalyticsCamp and a Meetup?

A typical Meetup event is just that: a meetup. Typically without any agenda, sometimes with a speaker. We however follow the barcamp format where participants can propose and hold their own sessions (more democratic). We do nevertheless advertise and sometimes organise our events on meetup.com - mainly for the publicity.
